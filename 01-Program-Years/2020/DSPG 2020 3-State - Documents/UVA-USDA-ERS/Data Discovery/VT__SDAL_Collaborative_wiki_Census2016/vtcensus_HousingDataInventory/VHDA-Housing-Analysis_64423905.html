VT Census Case Studies : VHDA Housing Analysis

Created by saa795, last modified by molfe on 02 Jan 2016

Brief Overall Description of the Dataset:

“VHDA regularly conducts analyses of housing market and demographic trends impacting housing needs in the Commonwealth. These analytical assessments assist us in our organizational strategic and program planning. They also provide data that can assist broader state housing policy-making activities, and the activities of other housing stakeholders.”  

This data is not appropriate for our project because it is not based on specific housing properties.  I believe all of the housing data from these reports (the link to which is provided above) comes from the Census or the ACS and, when I went back a week later to try to look at the reports again, I was not able to access the reports.  I tried calling VHDA, and I could not get ahold of them.

Therefore, from what I read on their website, I will assume they do not have property or housing unit level data.  Furthermore, from my notes from when I was able to access the reports, I wrote down that the data was not collected consistently, and that there was only some data for 2013. Hence, these two question are not 'checked' in the screening questions.

VHDA does issue reports about their lending practices, but this did not seem to be something we would be interested in obtaining.  Therefore, for the purposes of this inventory, I will be focusing on the Census and ACS housing data they use.  

Link: http://www.vhda.com/about/Planning-Policy/Pages/VHDAHousingAnalysis.aspx#.VV-INk9Vikr

Purpose

  • What is the purpose of the organization collecting the data?

“VHDA is a self-supporting, not-for-profit organization created by the Commonwealth of Virginia in 1972, to help Virginians attain quality, affordable housing. VHDA provides mortgages, primarily for first-time homebuyers and developers of quality rental housing. We use no state taxpayer dollars, but raise money in the capital markets to fund our loans.”

  • Why is it collected and how does the organization use it? 

VHDA collects the data for its own lending purposes (the data “assists them in their organizational strategic and program planning”), and these “analytical assessments” “also provide data that can assist broader state housing policy-making activities and the activities of other housing stakeholders.”
